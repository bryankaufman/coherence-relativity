# Key Insights: Coherence Relativity vs Existing Approaches

**Status**: Complete comparison matrix
**Purpose**: Quick reference for how CR relates to each major approach

## Central Thesis

**Coherence relativity is to quantum measurement what special relativity is to spacetime.**

- Makes previously absolute concepts (state, coherence) frame-relative
- Provides geometric framework (coherence space Sigma instead of Minkowski space)
- Derives seemingly fundamental rule (Born rule instead of time dilation formula)
- Preserves dynamics (unitary evolution)
- Makes testable predictions (frame transformation effects)

## Approach-by-Approach Comparison

### 1. Gleason's Theorem (1957)
- **Gleason**: Non-contextuality over measurement bases -> Born rule (mathematical)
- **CR**: Frame-invariance over coherence frames -> Born rule (physical)
- **What CR adds**: Physical picture for non-contextuality. Non-contextuality *emerges* from coherence-frame invariance.
- **Analogy**: Gleason proves the formula; CR explains *why* the formula must hold.

### 2. Zurek's Envariance (2003-2005)
- **Zurek**: Entanglement symmetry (envariance) -> equal probabilities -> Born rule
- **CR**: Frame transformations already embody entanglement structure; geometrizes envariance
- **What CR adds**: Tensor field T_AB on Sigma determines *when* envariance holds. Envariance is frame-dependent (novel prediction).
- **Key extension**: Einselection = frame transformation dynamics F_coherent -> F_decoherent

### 3. Deutsch-Wallace Decision Theory (Many Worlds)
- **DW**: Rationality axioms in branching universe -> Born rule as credence
- **CR**: Geometric frame-invariance -> Born rule as invariant measure
- **What CR adds**: Ontological economy—one reality R, many descriptions psi_F (not many worlds)
- **Key difference**: DW needs many worlds ontology; CR needs only many *frames* of single reality

### 4. Decoherence Program (Zeh, Zurek, Joos)
- **Decoherence**: Explains interference suppression and classical emergence; *assumes* Born rule
- **CR**: *Derives* Born rule; explains decoherence as frame-relative, not absolute
- **What CR adds**: Completes the decoherence program by (1) deriving what it assumes, (2) showing decoherence is geometric transformation in Sigma, (3) predicting frame-relativity of coherence loss
- **Quantum Darwinism connection**: T_AB determines which histories achieve redundant encoding -> only Darwinian-selected branches survive as objective reality

### 5. Relational QM (Rovelli)
- **RQM**: States are observer-relative (philosophical reinterpretation)
- **CR**: States are frame-relative (mathematical framework with Sigma, T_AB)
- **What CR adds**: Makes RQM rigorous with geometry, derives Born rule, makes testable predictions
- **Analogy**: RQM says "simultaneity is relative"; CR provides the Lorentz transformations

### 6. QBism (Fuchs, Caves, Schack)
- **QBism**: Quantum states = subjective beliefs; Born rule = coherence of beliefs
- **CR**: Quantum states = objective descriptions in frames; Born rule = geometric invariant
- **What CR adds**: Realist alternative—frames are physical (like SR reference frames), not mental. Born measure is objective, not normative.
- **Key difference**: QBist probabilities are "in the agent's head"; CR probabilities are geometric features of Sigma

### 7. Objective Collapse Models (GRW, CSL, Diosi-Penrose)
- **Collapse**: Modify Schrodinger dynamics with stochastic terms -> physical collapse
- **CR**: Keep unitary dynamics; "collapse" = frame transformation, not dynamical evolution
- **What CR adds**: No modified dynamics needed. Different experimental predictions (geometric scaling vs mass scaling, frame-transformation hysteresis vs spontaneous localization).
- **Testable distinction**: CR predicts non-linear visibility, hysteresis, curvature effects; collapse models predict mass-dependent decoherence rates

### 8. Recent Operational Reconstructions (Masanes et al. 2019)
- **Masanes**: Operational axioms -> Born rule is uniquely selected
- **CR**: Geometric frame-invariance axioms -> Born rule is uniquely selected
- **What CR adds**: Physical picture (Sigma, T_AB, frame transformations) rather than abstract operational constraints
- **Shared insight**: Born rule is tightly constrained by structural principles

## What CR Does NOT Claim

- Existing approaches are "wrong"
- CR is the "final theory"
- Standard QM predictions are incorrect in everyday regimes
- Many Worlds is impossible (just unnecessary for CR's explanatory goals)

CR provides a geometric framework that *unifies* insights from multiple approaches, like SR unified Newtonian mechanics with electromagnetism.
