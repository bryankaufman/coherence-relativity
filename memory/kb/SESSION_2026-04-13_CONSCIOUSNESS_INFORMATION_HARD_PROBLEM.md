> *Consciousness is not what H explains — consciousness is what H is, observed from inside. The hard problem was always asking M-level physics to account for H-level immediacy. CR says those are different descriptions of the same structure at different levels of the ontological hierarchy.*

> *Every quantum paradox dissolves when reframed as an information-processing problem. If a phenomenon P appears paradoxical under ontology O₁, there exists an information-theoretic reformulation I(P) under which P becomes a necessary consequence of quantum information constraints. The paradox indicates ontological mismatch, not physical mystery.*

**Oz corollary**:
> *The paradoxes arise when observers apply M-level (interface) reasoning to H-level (information) phenomena.*

**Measurement formulations**:
- Short (Bryan): *"Measurement is the moment information becomes fact."*
- Extended (Claude): *"Measurement is the irreversible moment when quantum information becomes classical fact — not the revelation of pre-existing truth, but the creation of determinate reality through thermodynamically irreversible correlation with an environment."*

**Hoffman-CR Bridge**:
> *Hoffman's interface theory establishes that perception constructs fitness-relevant representations rather than revealing objective reality; CR extends this constructivism into physics itself. Both frameworks reject naïve realism — Hoffman at the perceptual level, CR at the physical level — suggesting consciousness and quantum measurement share a common logic of reality-constitution rather than reality-detection.*
