Bryan Kaufman
Independent Researcher

 

ABSTRACT:

We propose that the quantum measurement problem is a frame problem, resolved by recognizing that coherence is relative to a choice of coherence frame---analogous to the relativity of simultaneity in special relativity. We introduce coherence relativity, a geometric framework in which physical reality is described on a joint manifold M   Sigma, where M is emergent spacetime and Sigma is a coherence manifold whose points represent distinct observational perspectives. In this framework, wave function collapse is not a dynamical process but a transformation between coherence frames induced by measurement interactions. We derive the Born rule---that measurement probabilities equal squared amplitudes---as the unique probability measure invariant under all allowed coherence-frame transformations, given additivity and continuity. This derivation unifies insights from Gleason's theorem, Zurek's envariance, and operational reconstruction programs within a single geometric structure. We demonstrate the framework with the double-slit experiment in two coherence frames and identify six testable predictions distinguishing coherence relativity from standard quantum mechanics: non-linear visibility scaling, frame-transformation hysteresis, coherence-space curvature effects, modified gravitational decoherence, measurable transformation timescales, and coherence revival. The most accessible predictions---erasure hysteresis and transformation timescales---are testable with current superconducting qubit and trapped ion platforms.

## Introduction

### The Measurement Problem as a Frame Problem

The quantum measurement problem---how definite outcomes emerge from superposed states---has persisted for nearly a century despite the extraordinary empirical success of quantum mechanics. At its core lies a conceptual asymmetry: the formalism describes systems evolving unitarily, yet observations yield definite results governed by the Born rule, p_k = | _k|^2. The standard resolution appends a measurement postulate that operates outside the unitary framework, creating what Penrose called ``the most important open problem in physics'' and what Bell termed ``the shifty split'' between quantum and classical domains.

Attempts to resolve this tension have proceeded along several lines. Gleason's theorem~[ref] showed the Born rule follows from non-contextuality, but left its physical origin unexplained. Zurek's envariance~[ref] derived probabilities from entanglement symmetry, though critics note potential circularity. The Deutsch--Wallace approach~[ref] obtained Born weights from rationality axioms in an Everettian universe. The decoherence program~[ref] explained interference suppression while assuming the Born rule. Relational quantum mechanics~[ref] and QBism~[ref] reinterpret the formalism without deriving probabilities. Objective collapse models~[ref] modify the dynamics itself.

Each captures an essential aspect. Yet none simultaneously provides: (i)~a derivation of the Born rule from physical principles, (ii)~a geometric framework explaining *why* those principles hold, (iii)~testable predictions, and (iv)~ontological clarity about frame-invariant content.

### Coherence as Relativity

We propose that the measurement problem is not a dynamical problem requiring new physics, but a *frame problem*---analogous to the shift from Newtonian absolute time to the relativity of simultaneity.

The central idea: **coherence is frame-relative**. What appears as a definite outcome in one coherence frame appears as a coherent superposition in another, just as events simultaneous in one inertial frame are non-simultaneous in another. There is no absolute fact about whether a system ``has collapsed.''

We formalize this through four elements:

-  **Coherence space~Sigma**: A manifold whose points represent coherence frames. Physical reality is described on M   Sigma.
-  **Frame transformations**: Maps U_{F   F'} between coherence frames, analogous to Lorentz transformations.
-  **Born rule as frame-invariant measure**: Requiring invariance under all allowed frame transformations, additivity, and continuity uniquely fixes  ( _i) = |c_i|^2.
-  **Tensor field T_AB**: A field on M   Sigma encoding the information geometry of coherence frames.

## Coherence Frames and the Geometry of Quantum Description

### Definitions

**Coherence Space:**
Let H be a Hilbert space of dimension~d. The **coherence space**~Sigma is the space of orthonormal bases of~H, modulo physically irrelevant phase redundancies:

Sigma = U(d) / T^d,

where U(d) is the unitary group and T^d is the maximal torus.

**Coherence Frame:**
A **coherence frame**~F   Sigma is a choice of preferred orthonormal basis \{i_F\} together with a specification of which degrees of freedom encode definiteness.

**State Representation:**
For physical reality~R, the state in frame~F is

 _F =  _i c_i^{(F)} i_F,

where the coefficients c_i^{(F)} depend on the frame. The same~R has different representations in different frames:  _F(R)    _{F'}(R) in general.

### Examples

**Single qubit.** For d=2, Sigma is parameterized by the Bloch sphere. The frame F_z corresponds to \{0, 1\}; the frame F_x to \{+, -\}. A state   =  0 +  1 in F_z becomes   =  + {2}+ +  - {2}- in F_x. Coherence is not a property of the state alone but of the state-in-a-frame.

**Double slit.** The interference frame F_int has basis states that are path superpositions; the which-path frame F_wp has path eigenstates. Both describe the same setup (Sec.~[ref]).

**Bipartite system.** For H_A   H_B, the global frame F_{global} exhibits entanglement. The local frame F_A (tracing out~B) shows mixedness instead. Decoherence is reinterpreted as a frame change.

### Wigner's Friend

Wigner's friend performs a measurement inside a laboratory (frame~F_{friend}: state has collapsed to   or  ). Wigner, outside the lab, describes the joint system in F_{Wigner}: the friend--system composite remains in superposition. In coherence relativity, both descriptions are correct *in their respective frames*. There is no contradiction, just as there is no contradiction between observers disagreeing about simultaneity in SR.

### EPR and Bell Nonlocality

For an entangled pair in the singlet state, Alice's and Bob's local frames each see mixed states. The global frame sees entanglement. Bell inequality violation is a frame-invariant fact (it depends only on |\! | \!|^2 terms). The correlations are not ``caused'' by collapse but are geometric properties of the entangled state visible from any frame.

### Schr\"odinger's Cat

The cat-in-a-box scenario involves two frames: F_{outside} (cat in superposition) and F_{inside} (cat alive or dead). Opening the box is a frame transformation U_{F_{outside}   F_{inside}}, not a physical collapse. The cat's definite state exists in F_{inside} at all times; it simply isn't accessible from F_{outside} without the frame transformation.

## Discussion

### Resolution of the Measurement Problem

coherence relativity resolves the measurement problem by dissolving it: ``collapse'' is a frame transformation, not a dynamical process. The Born rule is not postulated but derived as the unique frame-invariant measure. Definite outcomes exist in appropriate coherence frames; superposition exists in others. There is no need to explain *when* or *how* collapse occurs, because collapse is not a physical event---it is a change of description.

### Quasipotential Landscape

The tensor field~T_AB yields, in the strong-coupling limit, a quasipotential V( ) on~Sigma (Fig.~[ref]). Minima of~V correspond to metastable classical frames (pointer states), and barriers determine transition rates. This provides a geometric explanation for einselection: environment-induced superselection is the dynamical relaxation to V-minima.

### Quantum Darwinism and Objectivity

coherence relativity naturally accommodates quantum Darwinism~[ref]: when multiple environment fragments independently acquire the same information about the system, they select a preferred coherence frame. In coherence relativity language, the redundant encoding of pointer-state information across environment fragments~[ref] corresponds to the narrowing of the quasipotential well around the classical frame as the number of monitoring fragments~N increases.

### Relationship to Existing Frameworks

coherence relativity is closest in spirit to relational quantum mechanics~[ref] but differs in three respects: (i)~Sigma has concrete geometric structure; (ii)~the Born rule is derived, not assumed; (iii)~the tensor field~T_AB provides dynamics absent in RQM.

Compared to QBism~[ref], coherence relativity is realist: frame-invariant content (Born measures, inner products, spectra) constitutes observer-independent physical fact.

Compared to many-worlds~[ref], coherence relativity posits one reality and many frames, not many worlds and one agent. The logical structure of the Born rule derivation parallels the Deutsch--Wallace approach, but replaces branching ontology with geometric frame-invariance.

The quantum reference frame (QRF) program~[ref] shares coherence relativity's emphasis on frame-dependence: Giacomini et al.\ showed that entanglement and superposition are QRF-dependent~[ref], and perspective-neutral approaches~[ref] seek frame-invariant structures. coherence relativity differs in scope: QRFs transform between quantum systems serving as references, while coherence frames transform between *descriptive bases* for a fixed system. The coherence space~Sigma provides a global geometric arena absent in QRF treatments, and the Born rule emerges as its unique invariant measure rather than being assumed.

## Conclusion

We have introduced coherence relativity, a geometric framework in which:

-  Quantum coherence is frame-relative, like simultaneity in special relativity.
-  The Born rule  _F(i) = |c_i|^2 is the unique frame-invariant probability measure on the coherence manifold~Sigma.
-  Wave function collapse is a frame transformation, not a dynamical process.
-  The information geometry of~Sigma, encoded in the Fubini--Study metric and the tensor field~T_AB, generates a quasipotential landscape explaining einselection and classical emergence.

The framework unifies insights from Gleason, Zurek, and operational reconstruction programs while making six testable predictions. The most accessible---frame-transformation hysteresis and measurable transformation timescales---are within reach of current superconducting qubit and trapped ion experiments.
