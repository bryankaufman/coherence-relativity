Significance and Novelty

This manuscript addresses the quantum measurement problem through a novel geometric framework that treats coherence as frame-relative—analogous to the relativity of simultaneity in special relativity. The work makes three principal contributions:

  1. Geometric Framework: We introduce coherence space Σ = U(d)/T^d as a physical arena where different observational perspectives correspond to distinct points. Physical reality is described on the joint manifold M × Σ, where M is spacetime and Σ encodes coherence frames. This provides a geometric foundation for understanding why measurement outcomes appear definite.

  2. Born Rule Derivation: We derive the Born rule μ(ψ_i) = |c_i|² as the unique probability measure invariant under coherence-frame transformations (relabelings and splittings), given additivity and continuity. This unifies insights from Gleason’s theorem, Zurek’s envariance, and operational reconstruction programs within a single geometric structure, while clarifying the physical origin of the invariance principle.

  3. Testable Predictions: Unlike purely interpretational approaches, coherence relativity makes six experimentally testable predictions distinguishing it from standard quantum mechanics:

    • Non-linear visibility scaling with detector strength
    • Frame-transformation hysteresis in erasure experiments
    • Coherence-space curvature effects (measured via Fubini-Study metric)
    • Modified gravitational decoherence rates
    • Measurable transformation timescales (~10-100 μs for superconducting qubits)
    • Coherence revival under specific conditions

The most accessible predictions—erasure hysteresis and transformation timescales—are testable with current superconducting qubit and trapped ion platforms.

Relationship to Prior Work

The framework builds on but differs fundamentally from: - Decoherence theory: We provide the geometric structure explaining why pointer bases emerge, not just how interference is suppressed - Many-worlds/Everett: We avoid ontological proliferation while maintaining unitary evolution; frame transformations are geometric, not branching events - Relational QM & QBism: We derive rather than assume the Born rule, and identify frame-invariant ontological content - Collapse models: No modification of quantum dynamics; “collapse” is reinterpreted as frame transformation

Why Foundations of Physics?

This work is ideal for Foundations of Physics because it: - Addresses a central foundational question (the measurement problem) - Proposes a mathematically rigorous geometric framework - Derives rather than postulates the Born rule - Makes falsifiable experimental predictions - Provides conceptual clarity about quantum ontology - Connects foundational questions to information geometry and differential geometry

The manuscript is self-contained, includes 8 publication-quality figures, and provides detailed calculations in appendices. This is planned as Paper I in a series; Paper II will develop the dynamics on M × Σ and the quasipotential formalism in detail.
