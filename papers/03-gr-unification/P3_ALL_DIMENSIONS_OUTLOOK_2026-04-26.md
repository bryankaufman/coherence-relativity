## §2. The Generalization Conjecture

The three-stage mediated-emergence pattern (see Paper 2C §8) is:
1. large kinematical structure
2. coherence/entanglement mediator
3. derived effective coordinate

The conjecture is that **every effective coordinate** of the macroscopic world (time, three spatial dimensions, and any extra dimensions such as KCR's r or holographic depth z) is derivable from this pattern, where different mediators generate different coordinates.

Concretely, three coordinate types are conjectured to follow from three distinct mediator geometries:

### 2.1 Temporal coordinate (M's time direction)

**Naive slogan:** "Time is just entropy increase" or "time flows from the quantum dynamics."

**Proposed mediator:** The moving fact horizon ∂M(t) (Paper 3 §3). The fact horizon is the boundary between the accumulated irreversible record M and the unresolved coherosphere. Its motion — as decoherence events continually enlarge M — introduces a preferred direction on M that breaks the time-reversal symmetry of the underlying Schrödinger dynamics.

**Proposed derived coordinate:** A time parameter t defined by the progression of ∂M(t). Not "thermodynamic time" (statistical) but "ontological time" (the rate at which facts become irreversible on M).

**Status:** Conceptually motivated by Paper 3 §3 ("M is the past"). Not yet a derivation of the time-coordinate metric from Σ-side data. Open problem: whether the SCF-induced flow on Σ generates a foliation of M × Σ with compatible time structure.

**Connects to:** Connes' noncommutative time from the Tomita–Takesaki modular flow; Rovelli's thermal time hypothesis; Isham's history projection operators — all attempts to extract a time direction from purely quantum data.

### 2.2 Three spatial dimensions (M's spatial geometry)

**Naive slogan:** "Space is entanglement" (Van Raamsdonk) or "space emerges from information geometry" (Cao–Carroll–Michalakis).

**Proposed mediator:** The basin-measure flow on Σ together with the holographic projection 𝒫_holo: M × Σ → M × {ξ_0(x)} (Paper 2C §RC-1). The Riemannian geometry on the spatial slices of M is conjectured to be reconstructable from the entanglement/coherence structure of Σ-states, generalizing the Cao–Carroll–Michalakis (CCM) reconstruction beyond their redundancy-state class assumption.

**Proposed derived coordinates:** The three spatial coordinates of M as a Riemannian 3-manifold, recovered via a multidimensional-scaling-type procedure on the Σ-side coherence-frame distances (FS/Bures metric) under 𝒫_holo.

**Status:** This is the Paper 4 (Riemannian sub-problem) of the PAPER7_SCOPE constructive reconstruction arc. It is a research direction, not a current result. The CCM program provides the closest published partial realization.

**Key open subproblem:** The CCM program uses mutual information between subsystems; the CR analog uses FS/Bures coherence-frame distances. Whether these agree in a suitable limit is an explicit test that would either validate or constrain the spatial-dimension story.

### 2.3 Extra dimensions (r and holographic depth)

**Already derived (for KCR):** The decoherence-depth r is the one explicitly derived example in the current series (Paper 2B/2C, ACTION1_WARPFACTOR_RESOLUTION). It comes from the CP¹ / Hopf Σ-side topology → M × Σ EOMs → A'' = -k²A → r_max = π/(2√2).

**Already mapped (for holographic z):** The AdS radial coordinate z emerges from the MERA/cMERA entanglement-renormalization mediator applied to the boundary Hilbert space (Paper 2C §8, METHODOLOGICAL_PRINCIPLE_MEDIATED_EMERGENCE, §C).

**Status:** These two are the concrete anchors of the conjecture. All other dimensions (time, space) are analogous but not yet derived.

## §4. Relationship to Existing Emergent-Spacetime Programs

The conjecture as stated is compatible with, but distinct from, several existing programs:

**"It from qubit" / spacetime from entanglement (Ryu–Takayanagi, Van Raamsdonk, ER=EPR):**
These programs establish that geometric structure is deeply tied to quantum entanglement. The CR conjecture adds: (a) a specific mediator object (Σ and M × Σ) that is not the boundary Hilbert space; (b) an explicit decoherence mechanism (pointer-sector selection, Markov suppression) that performs the "dimensional reduction"; (c) an ontological account (fact horizon, H ⊋ M) for why a particular macroscopic M is realized at all.

**CCM (Cao–Carroll–Michalakis) — "space from Hilbert space":**
The closest spatial analog. CR would extend their MDS/mutual-information approach to FS/Bures coherence-frame distances, generalize beyond redundancy-state classes, and add T_{MΣ} as the mediator between Σ-geometry and M-geometry. The CCM reconstruction is the Riemannian sub-problem (Paper 4 in the seven-paper arc).

**Connes–Chamseddine almost-commutative models:**
The almost-commutative spectral triple M × F has structural similarity to M × Σ (both are product geometries with an internal space). The content differs: F carries gauge charges; Σ carries coherence frames. The time coordinate in Connes' program comes from the Tomita–Takesaki modular automorphism, which is an algebraic analog of the ontological time proposed here from ∂M(t). The analogy is formal but suggestive.

**Causal set approaches:**
The fact-horizon ontology of Paper 3 (M = accumulated irreversible records; ∂M = decoherence front) is structurally similar to a causal set: M is built element by element as decoherence events occur, and the causal structure is determined by which facts are in M at each stage. The CR framework has a continuous geometry on M × Σ and a dynamical ∂M, rather than a discrete causal set, but the construction shares the "spacetime as accumulated events" intuition.

## §5. What Would Falsify the Conjecture

The conjecture is testable in principle. It would be falsified by any of:
- A proof that Σ-side coherence-frame distances cannot reproduce the correct spatial geometry of M (e.g., wrong dimension count, wrong topology) even in a suitable limit.
- A demonstration that the ∂M(t) dynamics do not generate a globally hyperbolic time foliation compatible with the spatial geometry derived in (1).
- Inconsistency between the r-coordinate derived in KCR and the spatial coordinates derived from the Riemannian sub-problem — i.e., if the two derivations produce incompatible geometric structures on M.
- A proof that the separately derived coordinates yield a Riemannian, not Lorentzian, signature.

Each of these is a concrete research question for Papers 4–6.
