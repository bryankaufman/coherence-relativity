## 1) Scope and Role in the Series
Paper 3 establishes emergence dynamics on `M×Σ`.

Working claim: holography is not an independent add-on, but a representational equivalence layer built on the emergence backbone.

## 2) Dual Representation Principle
Let `M×Σ` be the full description space. Then an admissible duality map `𝒟` relates:
- a bulk-style representation emphasizing effective geometric dynamics, and
- a boundary-style representation emphasizing encoded informational structure.

`𝒟` should preserve physically admissible predictions while permitting nontrivial redistribution between geometric and informational degrees of freedom.

## 3) Invariants Under Duality
Candidate invariant classes under `𝒟`:
- normalization/Born consistency constraints,
- total accessible information capacity (global),
- causal consistency of effective observables in the relevant regime.

Objects expected to transform (not remain pointwise invariant):
- local metric form,
- locality manifestness,
- entropy density placement across sectors.

## 4) Entropy Flow as Encoding Flow
In Paper 3 language, entropy tracks spent distinguishability along decoherence flow.  
In Paper 4 language, the same process is re-read as encoding migration between dual descriptions:
- coarse-graining in one representation corresponds to compression/relabeling in the other,
- apparent irreversibility is tied to representational projection plus sector truncation.

This gives a concrete bridge between thermodynamic arrow statements and duality maps.

## 5) Boundary/Bulk Reading of `M×Σ`
Minimal proposal:
- `Σ`-dominant formulation: distinguishability geometry and frame transition structure are primary;
- `M`-dominant formulation: effective spacetime/gravitational geometry is primary;
- cross-term structure (`T_MΣ` in Paper 2 notation) sets how strongly the two descriptions are mutually entangled.

In this view, “bulk emergence” is the late-stage effective face of an earlier mixed geometric-information regime.

## 6) Testable/Constrainable Consequences
This extension should generate constraints, not just interpretation:
- dual description must reproduce Paper 3 suppression behavior in overlapping regimes,
- entropy-accounting consistency must hold across maps,
- level-mismatch diagnostics should be representation-independent.

Failure of any of the above falsifies a proposed duality map even if it is mathematically elegant.
