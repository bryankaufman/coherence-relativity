### Deliverable 3: Naturality Preservation Lemma + No-Go Covariance Theorem

This is the mathematical core of Phase 1.

#### Lemma (Naturality Preservation Under Banach Fixed Point)

State and prove the following lemma (or a corrected version if the statement as given needs refinement):

> **Lemma NP.** *Let F: {Riemannian/Kähler manifolds with SU(d) structure} → {Natural bundles} be a natural transformation (in the sense of Kolar-Michor-Slovak Chapter VI) satisfying the admissibility conditions of Definition [D2 above]. Suppose the SCF equation T = F[g_M(T), g_Σ(T), C] has a unique solution T* in a Banach ball B_R(T₀) (via a Banach contraction argument with contraction constant κ < 1). Then T* is also a natural object: T* transforms as T* → U · T* · U⁻¹ under all U ∈ SU(d) acting on C and Σ.*

**Proof strategy to formalize:** The argument should go through these steps:
1. F is natural ⟹ F commutes with the SU(d) action: F(U · ·) = U · F(·) · U⁻¹
2. The SU(d) action on the space of tensors over M × Σ is an isometry of the Banach space (the metric is SU(d)-invariant since g_Σ = g^FS is SU(d)-invariant)
3. Applying U to both sides of T* = F[g_M(T*), g_Σ(T*), C]: U · T* = U · F[…] = F[U · g_M · U⁻¹, g_Σ, U · C] · U = F[g_M, g_Σ, U · C] · U (using SU(d)-invariance of g_Σ and inertness of g_M)
4. But then U · T* also satisfies the SCF equation with context U · C — and by uniqueness of the fixed point (Banach), U · T* is the UNIQUE fixed point for the rotated context
5. In the context-neutral formulation (fixing the SU(d)-orbit of C, not C itself), this means T* must transform equivariantly: T*(U · C) = U · T*(C) · U⁻¹
6. This is exactly the COV postulate.

Identify any gaps in this argument. If a step requires an additional hypothesis, state it explicitly.
