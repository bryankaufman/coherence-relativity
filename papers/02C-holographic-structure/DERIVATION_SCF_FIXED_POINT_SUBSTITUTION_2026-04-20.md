## §2. Direct Substitution

Freeze the \(\Sigma\)-geometry to Fubini–Study:

\[
g_\Sigma = g^{FS},
\qquad
\nabla_\Sigma = \nabla^{FS}.
\]

Then substitute \(T_{M\Sigma} = F = \nabla^{FS} + A_C\) into the SCF equation:

\[
\nabla^{FS} + A_C
\;=\;
\nabla^{FS}[g^{FS}] + A_C[g_M(\nabla^{FS} + A_C)].
\]

The \(\nabla^{FS}\) terms cancel, so the fixed-point condition reduces to

\[
\boxed{
A_C = A_C[g_M(F)].
}
\tag{2.1}
\]

This is the clean algebraic outcome of the substitution. The question is then how (2.1) is realized.

---

## §3. Case 1 — Constant Pointer Basis

Assume the pointer basis is constant along \(M\):

\[
\partial_\mu |B_j\rangle = 0
\qquad
\forall\, \mu \in TM.
\]

Then the \(M\)-direction components of the pointer connection vanish:

\[
(A_C)_\mu
=
\sum_j |\partial_\mu B_j\rangle\langle B_j|
=
0.
\]

So \(A_C\) is independent of the \(M\)-metric data, and

\[
A_C[g_M(F)] = A_C.
\]

Hence (2.1) is satisfied exactly:

\[
F = \nabla^{FS} + A_C
\]

is an exact SCF fixed point in the constant-basis branch.

**Verdict for Case 1:** ✅ verified.

---

## §4. Case 2 — Varying Pointer Basis

Now allow the pointer basis to vary over \(M\). Then

\[
(A_C)_\mu
=
\sum_j |\partial_\mu B_j\rangle\langle B_j|
\neq 0,
\]

so the coherence field sources \(M\)-side back-reaction through the stress tensor built from

\[
D_\mu = \partial_\mu + (A_C)_\mu.
\]

In this branch, the substitution closes if and only if the \(M\)-curvature of the pointer connection equals the induced back-reaction:

\[
\boxed{
F^M_{A_C,\mu\nu}
=
\Omega_{\mu\nu}[|\psi\rangle, g_M].
}
\tag{4.1}
\]

Here

\[
F^M_{A_C,\mu\nu}
=
\partial_\mu (A_C)_\nu
- \partial_\nu (A_C)_\mu
+ [(A_C)_\mu,(A_C)_\nu]
\]

is the \(M\)-curvature of the Berry/pointer connection, and \(\Omega_{\mu\nu}[|\psi\rangle, g_M]\) is the back-reaction 2-form generated by the coherence field on \(M\).

Equation (4.1) is the geometric **einselection equation**: the pointer basis is self-consistent exactly when its spacetime rotation is stabilized by the back-reaction it sources.

**Verdict for Case 2:** ⚠️ nontrivial branch identified; SCF is equivalent to einselection.

## §5. Adiabatic and Finite-Back-Reaction Regimes

### Adiabatic limit

When the pointer basis varies slowly and the \(M\)-side back-reaction is negligible, both sides of (4.1) are small, so the equation reduces to \(0 = 0\) to leading order.

**Adiabatic verdict:** ✅ fixed point exists trivially.

### Finite back-reaction

For non-negligible back-reaction, existence is no longer automatic. The updated working draft closes this branch by constructing a Banach fixed-point map on the space of connections.

## §6. Banach Fixed-Point Closure in the QM Regime

Let

\[
X = W^{1,p}(M, T^*M \otimes \mathfrak{u}(d)),
\qquad
p > 4,
\]

and work on a closed ball \(B_R(A_0) \subset X\) around the background Berry connection \(A_0\).

The updated draft factors the einselection loop into

\[
\Phi := YM \circ Q \circ E,
\]

where:

- \(E\) maps \(A_C\) to the back-reacted metric \(g_M\),
- \(Q\) maps \(g_M\) to the induced Fubini–Study 2-form \(\Omega\),
- \(YM\) reconstructs a connection from that curvature data.

With the corresponding Lipschitz bounds, the contraction constant is

\[
\kappa := \epsilon\, C_E C_Q C_{YM}.
\]

If

\[
\epsilon < \epsilon^*
:=
(C_E C_Q C_{YM})^{-1},
\]

then \(\kappa < 1\), \(\Phi\) is a contraction on \(B_R(A_0)\), and Banach's theorem gives a unique fixed point

\[
A_C^* \in B_R(A_0)
\]

such that

\[
F^M_{A_C^*,\mu\nu}
=
\Omega_{\mu\nu}[|\psi\rangle, g_M(A_C^*)].
\]

This closes local existence for the varying-basis branch in the nondegenerate QM regime.

Important scope limit:

- this is **local** uniqueness inside the Banach ball, not gauge-uniqueness of the full theory;
- the argument is presently stated in the QM regime, with QFT translation still open;
- the draft gives local existence, not a global continuation theorem on all of \(M\).
