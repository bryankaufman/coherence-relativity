## §0. Why Paper 7, Not Paper 3 or 4

The constructive reconstruction map 𝓕: (H, ∂𝒞, T_{AB}) → (M, g_{μν}, {Φ_a}) is the deepest open problem in HCR and is, arguably, an open problem in the broader "spacetime from quantum information" literature. Treating it as Paper 7 — terminal — rather than absorbing it into Paper 3 (where 𝓕 is currently *named* as a long-range program) reflects three honest assessments:

1. **Depth:** 𝓕 requires reconstructing not just spatial geometry but Lorentzian signature, causality, dynamics, and matter content from boundary Hilbert-space data. No published program in any framework has done this.
2. **Dependency:** 𝓕 depends on closures that themselves are intermediate papers (Papers 4–6, sketched in §6 below).
3. **Risk discipline:** packaging 𝓕 as terminal allows the rest of the series to ship without the deepest item gating publication. Paper 3 can name 𝓕; Paper 7 attempts it.

This document is what 𝓕 looks like *from where we currently stand*. It is a research plan, not a result.

## §1. The 𝓕 Problem Statement

> **Definition (informal).** Let H be a separable Hilbert space, ∂𝒞 a decoherence boundary structure on H (the fact horizon's algebraic representative), and T_{AB} the coherence cross-term tensor on M × Σ_coh(H). The constructive reconstruction map is a procedure
>
>   𝓕: (H, ∂𝒞, T_{AB}) → (M, g_{μν}, {Φ_a})
>
> producing a Lorentzian 4-manifold M with metric g_{μν} and matter content {Φ_a} such that:
>
> (a) the projected coherence cross-term reproduces the effective bulk stress T^{(eff)}_{μν};
> (b) the metric satisfies the field equations sourced by that stress;
> (c) the matter content reproduces, via Φ: M × Σ → ℙ(H), the Born-rule statistics of the input Hilbert state structure;
> (d) the fact horizon ∂M is identified with the image of ∂𝒞 under the construction.

Three sharpenings worth making explicit:

- **Constructive, not existential.** The goal is not "such an M exists for some Hilbert data" but "given Hilbert data, here is the procedure that produces M, g, Φ_a."
- **Lorentzian, not Riemannian.** Causal structure must emerge.
- **Dynamic, not static.** The metric is reconstructed dynamically, not as a snapshot.

## §2. What HCR Currently Gives Us Toward 𝓕

The framework as it stands at 2026-04 closes a *fraction* of 𝓕. Itemized:

### ✅ Already in hand

- **Boundary fibration as data locus.** The synthesis theorem (per `SYNTHESIS_EMANATION_INSTANTIATION_LOCUS_2026-04-26.md`) identifies ∂M × Σ ⊂ M × Σ as the locus of emanation–instantiation. This tells us *where* the reconstruction data lives, even if not *how* to invert it.
- **Mediator tensor.** T_{MΣ} is a derived geometric object on M × Σ, not a postulated coupling. Its existence and SCF/COV constraints are settled (modulo G1).
- **Boundary-to-bulk dictionary at the stress level.** RC-1 (Paper 2C §RC1) gives S^{bdry}_M = λ_{bdry} ∫_{∂M} √(-γ) Tr(T_M T_M†) and T^{(eff)}_{μν} = λ |T_M|² Π_{μν} δ_⊥(x, ∂M). This is the *bulk metric source side* of 𝓕, derived.
- **Holographic projection.** 𝒫_holo: M × Σ → M × {ξ_0(x)} is defined and consistent. ξ_0 = arg max λ provides a section of the bundle.
- **H ⊋ M ontology.** The fact horizon ∂M is primitive (Paper 3 §1). The CMB last-scattering surface is identified with ∂M at t = t_rec.
- **Born side.** The Σ-side coherence data on rays of H reproduces Born statistics via the SCF/COV/Born chain (modulo G1, Wilczek–Zee, QFT translation).

### ⚠️ Partial / sketch only

- **Existence-and-uniqueness shape for 𝓕.** The synthesis claims an existence-and-uniqueness *shape* for 𝓕 by identifying the boundary data locus, but does not prove existence-and-uniqueness as a theorem.
- **Matter content.** {Φ_a} appears in the codomain of 𝓕 but its reconstruction from T_{MΣ} is not specified. Currently a black box.
- **Lorentzian structure.** Time direction is generated by the moving fact horizon ∂M(t) per Paper 3, but how Lorentzian *signature* on g_{μν} arises from boundary data is not yet shown.

### ❌ Not addressed

- No constructive procedure for going from (H, ∂𝒞, T_{AB}) to a manifold.
- No analog of HKLL bulk-reconstruction theorems for HCR.
- No causality / hyperbolicity argument from Σ-side data.
- No representation-theoretic decomposition of T_{MΣ} that would give individual matter fields.

**Realistic estimate of how much of 𝓕 is in hand:** ~30%, mostly on the *side conditions* that the reconstructed (M, g, Φ_a) must satisfy. The reconstruction *procedure* itself is ~5%.

## §3. Limitations and Blocks Remaining

### 3.1 Lorentzian vs Riemannian

The closest existing program — Cao–Carroll–Michalakis — reconstructs *Riemannian* spatial geometry from mutual information graph metrics via MDS. HCR needs *Lorentzian* spacetime. The signature-bearing structure on the input data is not yet identified.

**Conjectured resolution path:** the moving fact horizon ∂M(t) in Paper 3 introduces a preferred time direction; combined with the SCF-induced flow on Σ, this may generate a hyperbolic structure on the reconstructed manifold. Not derived. ⚠️ Even if this works, it's foliation-by-time slices, not full Lorentzian geometry — the 3+1 split is not the same as proper Lorentzian causality.

### 3.2 Matter content emergence

T_{MΣ} encodes coupling between coherence frames and spacetime, but extracting individual matter fields {Φ_a} from this single object requires a representation-theoretic decomposition that has not been performed. By analogy, in Connes–Chamseddine almost-commutative SM models, the matter content is read off the spectral triple's algebra; HCR has no analog yet.

**Conjectured resolution path:** decompose T_{MΣ} under the SU(d) action established in COV; the irreducible blocks should correspond to matter representations. Highly speculative.

### 3.3 Dynamics

Even granting an emergent (M, g_{μν}), the question of whether g satisfies the *dynamical* field equations sourced by T^{(eff)}_{μν} is open. This is not just consistency — it's reconstruction of dynamics from kinematic boundary data.

**Conjectured resolution path:** the SCF condition itself is dynamical (it's a fixed-point equation on the joint manifold). Lifting SCF dynamics on Σ to Einstein-equation-like dynamics on M is a candidate route, but the lift is not constructed.

### 3.4 No anchor theorem

Bulk reconstruction in AdS/CFT is anchored by HKLL. HCR has no analog — no theorem that says "given boundary data of this form, the bulk operator is uniquely reconstructed by this formula." Without an anchor, the reconstruction is unconstrained at the operational level.

**Conjectured resolution path:** an HKLL-analog for HCR would take Σ-side coherence operators to bulk fields via a smearing kernel built from T_{MΣ}. Not constructed.

### 3.5 Multi-scale / fact-horizon dynamics

∂M is moving (Paper 3). The reconstruction must therefore be time-resolved, not single-snapshot. Whether the construction commutes with ∂M-evolution (i.e., whether 𝓕 at t and 𝓕 at t' yield consistent metrics on overlapping regions) is open.

**Conjectured resolution path:** require 𝓕 to be functorial in t; impose consistency conditions across overlapping snapshots. Not formalized.

## §4. Outstanding Gaps

Beyond the limitations in §3, four gaps would need to be closed for 𝓕 to be a theorem:

1. **Domain definition.** What counts as "valid" Hilbert boundary data (H, ∂𝒞, T_{AB}) such that the reconstruction yields a well-formed Lorentzian manifold? Currently undefined.
2. **Equivalence/uniqueness up to diffeomorphism.** Two equivalent boundary data should reconstruct diffeomorphic manifolds. Statement of equivalence is missing.
3. **Inverse direction.** Is there a co-functor 𝓕^{-1} that takes (M, g_{μν}, {Φ_a}) and returns boundary data? In a fully holographic theory there should be. Status: open.
4. **Consistency with limits.** When restricted to AdS asymptotics, does 𝓕 reproduce HKLL? When restricted to CCM-style finite-d redundancy states, does it reproduce their MDS reconstruction? These are tests the theory must pass before being claimed.

## §5. Conjectured Path to Solution (Five Steps)

This is a research plan, not a sketch of a result. Each step is itself a paper (or substantial section of one).

### Step 1 — Riemannian sub-problem (Paper 4, conjectural)

Reconstruct spatial geometry from Σ-side coherence data using the basin-measure flow on Σ plus 𝒫_holo, generalizing Cao–Carroll–Michalakis beyond their redundancy-state class. **Output:** (Σ_R, g_{ij}^{(spatial)}) on a 3-manifold.

**Open subproblems:**
- Generalize CCM's MDS to coherence-frame data (not just mutual information).
- Identify the basin-measure flow as the right replacement for CCM's redundancy-class assumption.
- Bound the geometric distortion induced by the basin reduction.

### Step 2 — Add time / Lorentzian structure (Paper 5, conjectural)

Use the moving fact horizon ∂M(t) and the SCF-induced flow on Σ to generate a time direction and impose a hyperbolic structure compatible with Step 1's spatial geometry. **Output:** (M, g_{μν}) with Lorentzian signature.

**Open subproblems:**
- Show that ∂M(t)-foliation respects causality.
- Derive light-cone structure from Σ-side coherence boundaries.
- Establish that the resulting metric is globally hyperbolic.

### Step 3 — Recover dynamics (Paper 6, conjectural)

Show that the reconstructed metric satisfies field equations sourced by T^{(eff)}_{μν} from RC-1 — i.e., the SCF dynamics on Σ lift consistently to Einstein-like dynamics on M. **Output:** dynamical (M, g_{μν}), not just kinematic.

**Open subproblems:**
- Lift SCF fixed-point equation from Σ to M.
- Show the lifted equation reduces to Einstein equations (or HCR analog) sourced by T^{(eff)}_{μν}.
- Verify the lift is independent of the choice of foliation in Step 2.

### Step 4 — Matter sector (still Paper 6 or Paper 7 §)

Decompose T_{MΣ} under SU(d) into irreducible blocks; identify each block with a matter field {Φ_a}. **Output:** {Φ_a} as part of the reconstruction.

**Open subproblems:**
- Establish that the irreducible decomposition is finite (no infinite tower).
- Reconcile with the gauge structure in Connes–Chamseddine almost-commutative models.
- Predict matter spectrum from coherence-manifold dimension.

### Step 5 — Synthesis: 𝓕 itself (Paper 7)

Combine Steps 1–4 into the full constructive map. Verify against ER=EPR (in entanglement-pair limits) and AdS/CFT (in asymptotic limits) as consistency checks.

**Open subproblems:**
- Functorial composition of Steps 1–4.
- Uniqueness up to diffeomorphism.
- Reduction theorems against ER=EPR, AdS/CFT, CCM.
