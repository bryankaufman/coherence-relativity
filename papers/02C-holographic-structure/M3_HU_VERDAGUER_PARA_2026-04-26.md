## RC-1 Assumption A1 and Its Limitation

RC-1 derives T^(eff)_μν under Assumption A1: T_M (the coherence cross-term field on ∂M)
is treated as a *classical* background source. This is the standard semiclassical approximation
in which the quantum field T_M has a well-defined expectation value that sources the classical
metric. The assumption is physically motivated — it holds whenever the quantum fluctuations
of T_M are small relative to its mean — but it limits the domain of validity of the RC-1
result to the deterministic (mean-field) gravitational sector.

## Draft Paragraph (for 2C §8 Discussion)

**[INSERT in Paper 2C §8 Discussion, under a header like "Extensions and Limitations":]**

*Stochastic corrections to the RC-1 effective stress tensor.* The derivation of
T^(eff)_μν in §RC1.1–§RC1.2 treats the coherence cross-term T_M as a classical background
field (Assumption A1). When T_M's quantum fluctuations are retained, the back-reaction
on the metric acquires stochastic contributions whose two-point correlator is governed by
the quantum stress-tensor fluctuations ⟨T_M T_M⟩ − ⟨T_M⟩². The natural framework for
this extension is the Einstein–Langevin equation of semiclassical stochastic gravity
\cite{HuVerdaguer:2020}, in which the metric fluctuates around its mean-field value under
noise sourced by quantum stress-tensor correlators. A systematic treatment of these
stochastic corrections to the RC-1 result would (i) quantify the domain of validity of the
mean-field approximation, (ii) connect T^(eff)_μν to the gravitational decoherence
predictions of Paper 1 (Signature 4: modified gravitational decoherence), and (iii)
provide a noise-spectroscopy handle for distinguishing RC-1 from standard semiclassical
gravity in future experiments. This extension is outside the scope of the present paper
and is deferred to future work.

\bibitem{HuVerdaguer:2020} B. L. Hu and E. Verdaguer, \textit{Semiclassical and Stochastic
Gravity: Quantum Field Effects on Curved Spacetime} (Cambridge University Press, 2020).
Also: E. Verdaguer, "Stochastic semi-classical gravity," Living Rev. Relativity (various
years). arXiv:gr-qc/0205123 for an accessible review.

approximation that T_M is a classical background. When quantum fluctuations of T_M

Einstein–Langevin equation \cite{HuVerdaguer:2020}; computing these corrections
