## A: Diffeomorphism Action on T_M|_{∂M}

### Setup

Under an infinitesimal diffeomorphism ξ^μ on M, the Lie derivative of T_M^{μa} is:

  δ_ξ T_M^{μa} = £_ξ T_M^{μa} = ξ^ν ∇_ν T_M^{μa} − T_M^{νa} ∇_ν ξ^μ

Restricted to the boundary ∂M with tangential diffeomorphisms ξ^i (ξ^⊥ = 0 on ∂M):

  δ_ξ T_M^{ia}|_{∂M} = ξ^j ∂_j T_M^{ia} − T_M^{ja} ∂_j ξ^i

For this transformation to "kill" the ℓ=1 sector, we would need: for every T_M configuration with only ℓ=1 modes, there exists ξ^i such that δ_ξ T_M^{ia} = −T_M^{ia}, effectively setting the ℓ=1 modes to zero by gauge choice.

### Why This Fails

**Killing vectors are isometries, not gauge transformations of T_M.** The three ℓ=1 Killing vectors of S² (the rotation generators ∂_φ, sin φ ∂_θ − cot θ cos φ ∂_φ, cos φ ∂_θ + cot θ sin φ ∂_φ) satisfy:

  ∇_{(i} ξ_{j)} = 0   (Killing's equation)

This means they preserve the background metric g_{ij} of ∂M. They do NOT, however, generate gauge freedom for an arbitrary tensor field T_M that lives independently on ∂M.

**Gauge freedom requires: T_M has no physical content in the ℓ=1 sector.** This would require that the space of ℓ=1 configurations of T_M lies entirely in the image of the Lie derivative operator £_ξ acting on gauge parameters. For T_M a (1,1)-tensor on ∂M, the image of £_ξ under ℓ=1 vector fields ξ is a subspace of the space of (1,1)-tensors — but it does not, in general, span the entire ℓ=1 subspace of T_M configurations.

**Concrete obstruction**: A generic ℓ=1 mode of T_M^{ia} has 3 × (Σ-dimension) independent real components (one for each m = −1, 0, +1). The ℓ=1 Killing vectors of S² form a 3-dimensional space. The Lie derivative map £: (3D Killing) → (T_M deformations) maps into a 3D subspace of T_M deformations — but this subspace does not coincide with the full ℓ=1 sector of T_M unless T_M has a very special structure. For generic T_M, the ℓ=1 sector has more degrees of freedom than the Killing group can remove.

**Verdict A**: ❌ Diff(∂M) does not kill the ℓ=1 sector of T_M|_{∂M}. The Killing argument fails.

## B: Normal Component and Tangential Projection

### RC-1 Tangential Projector

The RC-1 effective stress tensor uses the tangential projector:

  T^(eff)_{μν} = λ |T_M|² Π_{μν} δ_⊥(x, ∂M)

where Π_{μν} = γ^{ij} e^α_i e^β_j g_{μα} g_{νβ} projects onto directions tangential to ∂M. This projects out T_M^{⊥a} (the normal component n_μ T_M^{μa}).

### Does This Remove ℓ=1?

The tangential projection removes the **normal** component of T_M (the ⊥-direction), not any particular angular harmonic. After projection, the tangential field T_M^{ia}|_{∂M} still carries all angular harmonics ℓ = 0, 1, 2, ..., including ℓ=1.

The projector acts in the **radial** direction (normal to ∂M in M), not in the angular directions on ∂M. These are orthogonal operations. The ℓ=1 angular modes are in the angular (S²) directions, and Π_{μν} does not touch them.

**Verdict B**: ❌ The RC-1 tangential projector does not suppress the ℓ=1 sector. It removes the normal (radial) component, not the dipole angular harmonic.

For ℓ=1, the tensor spherical harmonics T^{(E)}_{1m,ij} and T^{(B)}_{1m,ij} on S² are non-trivial and orthogonal to all ℓ≠1 modes. A T_M with only ℓ=1 content is:

  T_M^{ij} = Σ_m [b_{1m} T^{(E)}_{1m,ij} + c_{1m} T^{(B)}_{1m,ij}]

### Gauge Orbit Analysis

The Diff(S²) gauge orbit of a tensor field T on S² consists of all tensors of the form £_ξ T + T for diffeomorphisms ξ. For T_M = 0 (the vacuum), the gauge orbit is the image of £_ξ(0) = 0 — only zero itself. For non-zero T_M with ℓ=1 content, the question is whether T_M lies in the image of some £_ξ acting on a lower-ℓ seed.

**Obstruction**: A generic diffeomorphism ξ^i = Σ_{ℓ,m} [f_{ℓm} Ψ^i_{ℓm} + g_{ℓm} Φ^i_{ℓm}] generates a Lie derivative £_ξ h_{ij} (acting on the metric) that mixes ℓ modes. More precisely: the ℓ=1 tensor harmonics on S² include the "pure gauge" piece arising from £_{ξ_{ℓ=1}} h_{ij} (where ξ_{ℓ=1} is an ℓ=1 vector field). But T_M is not h_{ij} — it is an independent field not related to the metric by any equation at the kinematic level. Its ℓ=1 modes are not in the gauge orbit of Diff(S²) acting on T_M specifically.

**Verdict C**: ❌ The ℓ=1 modes of T_M^{ij}|_{∂M} are NOT in the gauge orbit of Diff(S²). They carry genuine physical degrees of freedom.
