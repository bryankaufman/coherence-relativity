## The Core Question

**Question:** Is the space of admissible, covariant SCF solutions connected under the SU(d) gauge action — i.e., does it form a single orbit — or does it split into multiple gauge-inequivalent orbits?

**Equivalent formulation:** Is the SCF connection F = ∇^FS + A_C unique up to gauge, or are there admissible covariant connections that are NOT gauge-equivalent to the canonical form?

**Why this matters for Born:** If there is a single orbit, all admissible SCF solutions generate the same basin weight W (up to gauge) → Born is unambiguous. If there are multiple orbits, each orbit may generate a distinct basin weight, and the paper must identify which orbit(s) are physical.

## Technical Setup

### The Space of Admissible Covariant Connections

From Phase 1 (Definition 2.1 in G1_PHASE1_RESULT), every admissible SCF functor is a natural SU(d)-equivariant map:

$$F: (M, \Sigma, C) \mapsto T_{M\Sigma} \in \Gamma(TM \otimes T^*\Sigma)$$

By Theorem G1-Cov, every such F satisfies COV. In terms of connections: T_MΣ corresponds to a connection on a principal bundle over M × Σ. The space of admissible connections is:

$$\mathcal{A}_{\rm adm} = \{F = \nabla^{\rm FS} + A : A \in \Omega^1(M, {\rm End}(\mathcal{H})),\; F \text{ is admissible and covariant}\}$$

where A is a 1-form on M valued in endomorphisms of the fiber ℋ.

The gauge group $\mathcal{G} = \mathcal{C}^\infty(M, SU(d))$ acts on $\mathcal{A}_{\rm adm}$ by conjugation:

$$g \cdot (∇^{FS} + A) = ∇^{FS} + g A g^{-1} - (dg) g^{-1}$$

The **moduli space** (space of gauge orbits) is $\mathcal{M}_{\rm adm} = \mathcal{A}_{\rm adm} / \mathcal{G}$.

**The question:** Is $\mathcal{M}_{\rm adm}$ a single point (single orbit), or does it have nontrivial structure?

### The Canonical Candidate and its Orbit

The canonical candidate is F₀ = ∇^FS + A_C where A_C = Σⱼ |dBⱼ⟩⟨Bⱼ| is the Berry/pointer connection.

**Key fact about A_C:** For any two pointer bases C₁ = {|B¹ⱼ⟩} and C₂ = {|B²ⱼ⟩} related by U ∈ SU(d) (i.e., C₂ = U · C₁), the connections are related by:

$$A_{C_2} = U \cdot A_{C_1} \cdot U^{-1} + \text{(flat correction)}$$

This is the standard gauge-transformation law for Berry connections. The "flat correction" term (dU)U⁻¹ accounts for the possibility of different connection representatives in the same gauge orbit.

**Orbit of F₀:** The gauge orbit of the canonical candidate under $\mathcal{G}$ is:

$$[F_0] = \{g \cdot F_0 : g \in \mathcal{G}\} = \{\nabla^{\rm FS} + g A_C g^{-1} - (dg)g^{-1} : g \in \mathcal{C}^\infty(M, SU(d))\}$$

**Question:** Does every admissible covariant connection lie in this orbit, or are there connections outside [F₀]?
