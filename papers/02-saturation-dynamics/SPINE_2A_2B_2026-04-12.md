## Narrative Arc — Paper 2A (Abstract Framework)

Paper 2A is self-contained and internally complete. Every claim is established at the framework
level — no specific geometry is required. The companion paper [2B] evaluates these claims on
the KCR-Cone, the first concrete geometry from derived compactification.

**Arc:**
1. Paper 1 established coherence geometry on Σ alone. Extending to M × Σ requires a
   state map Φ: M × Σ → PH and produces a new geometric object — the cross-term tensor
   T_MΣ = G_μa — encoding coupling between spacetime and frame sectors. (§2.1)

2. Controlling this coupling via the distinguishability field λ(x,ξ) separates the metric
   into M-sector, Σ-sector, and coupling blocks. The Euler-Lagrange equations yield
   frame-lag dynamics: spacetime acceleration sources coherence response. (§2.2)

3. The Markov ratio R_Markov provides a coordinate-invariant criterion for the quantum-to-
   classical transition: when R_Markov < ε, the Σ-sector decouples, the frame freezes, and
   semiclassical gravity is recovered. This is geometric classicality, not ℏ → 0. (§2.3/§2.6)

4. The Bohmian quantum potential Q = Q_BODC + Q_geom emerges from integrating out the Σ-sector
   via Born-Oppenheimer reduction. In the 1D two-slit model the match is algebraically exact
   (SymPy verified). Pilot wave theory is not postulated — it is derived. (§2.3)

5. The mixed-state Born rule extends Paper 1's pure-state result via purification and Naimark
   dilation. The left-right generator decomposition identifies the pointer sector criterion:
   Im(H_MΣ) = 0 iff pointer sector (Theorem 2.5.1). (§2.4, §2.5)

6. A century of compactification assumed topology. CR derives it: applying Paper 1's coherence
   axioms to the qubit (d=2) forces Σ = S². The minimal U(1) bundle over S² is the Hopf
   fibration S¹ → S³ → S², with c₁ = 1. Compactification follows from topology, not
   from a postulate. The string landscape of ~10^500 topologies collapses to countable
   c₁ ∈ ℤ. (§3.1, §3.2, §3.3)

7. The abstract Euler-Lagrange system on general M × Σ yields coupled geodesic equations
   and the frame-lag mechanism — a universal consequence of non-trivial T_MΣ. Standard
   evaluation methods expose convention dependencies that require a geometry; their resolution
   is the first task of Paper 2B. (§4)

8. Topology constrains C¹ regularity of the warp profile. In Randall-Sundrum models,
   regularity is absorbed by tunable junction conditions. In derived compactification,
   the smooth Hopf structure forbids distributional sources unless dynamically generated. (§4.4)

9. M × Σ admits a holographic interpretation: bulk = M × Σ, boundary = λ=1 locus,
   holographic direction = coherence parameter. A four-entry dictionary is established
   with three specific departures from AdS/CFT. The conjecture is well-posed but requires
   a concrete geometry to verify. (§5)
