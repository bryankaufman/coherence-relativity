## Abstract

We develop the coherence-frame formalism of Paper I to the joint manifold $M \times \Sigma$, where $M$ is spacetime and $\Sigma$ is the coherence-frame manifold equipped with the Fubini-Study/Bures metric. The cross-term tensor $T_{M\Sigma}$, derived from the Fubini-Study pullback, encodes the coupling between spacetime geometry and coherence-frame evolution. From this single geometric object we derive: (i) frame-lag equations of motion and a geometric Markov transition criterion that does not require $\hbar \to 0$; (ii) the Bohmian quantum potential as the Born-Oppenheimer effective potential arising from integrating out the $\Sigma$-sector, with an algebraically exact correspondence in the 1D two-slit model; (iii) a two-face Bures-Kähler metric $H_{M\Sigma} = G_{M\Sigma} + i\Omega_{M\Sigma}$ whose imaginary part vanishes precisely on the pointer sector (Theorem 2.5.1), giving a geometric derivation of einselection for invertible density operators; and (iv) a derivation that the compactification topology is forced by the coherence axioms to be the Hopf fibration $S^1 \hookrightarrow S^3 \to S^2$, collapsing the extra-dimensional landscape to a countable family. The resulting KK-Cone geometry satisfies the gravity-localization self-consistency condition with fifth-force coupling $\alpha_{\text{eff}} = 0$ exactly. A Casimir mechanism on the compact Hopf fiber yields a cosmological constant consistent with the observed value at a fiber radius $r^* \approx 22\,\mu\text{m}$ — a falsifiable prediction accessible to near-future short-range gravity experiments. A holographic structure conjecture identifies $\Sigma$ as the boundary and the coherence parameter as the holographic depth.
