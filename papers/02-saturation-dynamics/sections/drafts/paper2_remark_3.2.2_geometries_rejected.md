**Remark 3.2.2** (Geometries considered and rejected). The KK-Cone geometry adopted in this paper was selected from among several candidates after evaluating each against the three self-consistency conditions (SC1, SC2, SC3) and the requirement that the geometry follow from the coherence axioms rather than be postulated. We record the principal alternatives and their reasons for rejection.

*Five-dimensional de Sitter* ($\mathrm{dS}_5$) was rejected because de Sitter space has no stable holographic dual and no timelike boundary, making the identification $\Sigma = \partial M$ geometrically ill-defined. Nothing in the coherence axioms produces positive bulk curvature.

*Generic Calabi-Yau and string compactifications* are excluded by the logic of §3.2 itself: using a topology not derivable from the coherence axioms would reinstall the landscape problem the derived-compactification program is designed to solve.

*Randall-Sundrum RS1* (two-brane, $\mathbb{Z}_2$ orbifold) requires inserting both branes and the discrete symmetry by hand. These have no analogue in the coherence-frame axioms; RS1 cannot serve as the fundamental geometry, though elements of its stabilization mechanism may be useful for the radion problem (OP-5).

*Pure $\mathrm{AdS}_5$* was rejected for Papers 1--2 because its $SO(4,2)$ conformal symmetry has not been derived from the axioms. It is retained as a parallel-program candidate for Paper 4.

*Non-compact radial direction* (RS2-type with Hopf fiber) is logically clean — closing only what the axioms require — but eliminates the SC3 Casimir mechanism: zeta-function regularization of the vacuum energy requires a compact fiber. It also removes the primary observational prediction ($r^* \approx 22\,\mu\text{m}$).

*Warped time* (conformal AdS) was rejected because the unwarped time direction is a consequence of SC2, not an arbitrary choice — it is what gives $g_J^{\text{NR}} = 0$ exactly and closes the H1 fifth-force gate. Asymptotic warping in the UV remains a viable refinement in Paper 4.

The one acknowledged weakness of the current geometry is that the closure of $r$ at $L_*$ is motivated by SC3 rather than axiomatically forced. This is flagged explicitly as OP-5 (radion stabilization) and motivates the parallel geometry program in Paper 4, where the Hybrid geometry (asymptotically AdS with KK-Cone interior) and $\mathrm{AdS}_3 \times S^3$ are the primary candidates.
